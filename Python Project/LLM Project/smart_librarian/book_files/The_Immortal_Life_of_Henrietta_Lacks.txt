## Title: The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks

A Narrative Science / Bioethics book by Rebecca Skloot.

Target Audience: Adult

Main Themes: medical ethics, science, consent, inequality, bioethics, true story

Summary:
In 1951, doctors took Henrietta Lacks's cancer cells without consent; her 'HeLa' line transformed medicine. Decades later, her family learns what happened and grapples with exploitation, profit, and access. The book threads scientific breakthroughs with a human story about consent and inequality.
